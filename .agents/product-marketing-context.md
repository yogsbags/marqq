# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-11*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Marqq AI is a unified B2B marketing intelligence platform where 12 autonomous AI agents plan, execute, and optimise your marketing — with minimal human intervention.
**What it does:** Marqq AI gives B2B marketing teams a single platform to run lead intelligence, content creation, social campaigns, video generation, SEO, budget optimisation, and customer insights. A team of 12 named AI agents (Veena, Isha, Neel, Sam, Riya, Arjun, Maya, Kiran, Tara, Zara, Dev, Priya) work autonomously in the background, collaborating through a shared Marketing Knowledge Graph (MKG) and briefing the user each morning.
**Product category:** AI marketing platform / autonomous marketing OS
**Product type:** SaaS (B2B)
**Business model:** Subscription (pricing tiers TBD); workspace-based; per-company context model

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** B2B marketing teams are overwhelmed — too many channels, tools, and tasks for the headcount they have. They're reactive rather than strategic.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Point tools (Semrush, Hootsuite, HubSpot) require a human to operate each one — there's no connective tissue
- Agencies are slow, expensive, and don't have company-specific context
- Hiring is slow and doesn't scale fast enough for early-stage companies
- Generic AI tools (ChatGPT) require the user to prompt everything manually — there's no memory, no agents, no autonomy
**What it costs them:** Missed pipeline, wasted ad spend, inconsistent brand messaging, content gaps, slow response to market moves
**Emotional tension:** Feeling like you're always behind; fear of competitors moving faster; doubt that marketing is working at all

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** HubSpot Marketing Hub — falls short because it requires human operation of every feature; no autonomous agents; expensive for SMBs
**Direct:** Jasper / Copy.ai — falls short because it's only content generation with no agents, no memory, no strategy layer
**Secondary:** Marketing agencies — falls short because slow turnaround, high cost, low company context, can't run 24/7
**Secondary:** Semrush + Hootsuite + Apollo stack — falls short because fragmented; no shared intelligence between tools; high switching cost
**Indirect:** Hiring an in-house team — falls short because expensive, slow to ramp, doesn't scale overnight

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- 12 named specialist agents with distinct roles, memories, and skills — not a generic AI chatbot
- Shared Marketing Knowledge Graph (MKG) — agents share context so they build on each other's work
- Autonomous execution — agents run overnight, brief the user in the morning (Zara's daily synthesis)
- Product/service-aware — agents scope their work to a specific offer, not just the company
- India-aware — voice bot supports Indian English (Deepgram Nova 2), Indian audience ICP defaults, INR-denominated metrics
**How we do it differently:** Instead of the human operating each tool, 12 AI agents operate as a team — each with a specialty, a memory, and a role in the workflow. The user sets direction; agents execute.
**Why that's better:** Speed of execution without headcount cost; consistent brand voice across all outputs; agents compound knowledge over time via MKG
**Why customers choose us:** Full-stack execution in one place, autonomous operation, named agents feel like a real team

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "AI content will be generic / off-brand" | Agents are briefed with your company context, ICP, brand voice, and product specifics before every run. Output is anchored to your MKG, not a generic prompt. |
| "I don't trust AI to run my marketing unsupervised" | Agents brief you each morning (Zara's synthesis) and flag gaps/missing data. You stay in control of direction; agents handle execution. |
| "We already use HubSpot / agencies" | Marqq AI doesn't replace your CRM — it fills the execution gap above it. Agents produce the content, research, and campaigns that your team then approves. |

**Anti-persona:** Large enterprise marketing teams with dedicated specialists for each channel (they don't need agents to fill headcount gaps). Also: companies with no digital marketing presence or goals.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Frustration with managing too many disconnected tools; fatigue from manually prompting AI tools for every task; agencies missing deadlines or lacking industry context
**Pull:** The idea of a "marketing team that runs overnight"; named agents with memory feel more reliable than generic chatbots; single platform reduces tool sprawl
**Habit:** Existing workflows in HubSpot, spreadsheets, agency relationships; fear of migration cost
**Anxiety:** "Will it produce off-brand content?"; "What if the agents make a mistake I don't catch?"; "Is my data safe?"
